Biometric Data Privacy Statement

Effective: 2/10/26

Introduction

Allegheny County Roleplay ("ACRP", "We", "Us", or "Our"), uses biometric technologies as part of its age verification and assurance systems. This statement explains how biometric data is collected, used, stored, retained, and deleted when users complete facial age estimation and liveliness verification.

1. What We Collect

We collect a variety of biometric data, including but not limited to:

  • Facial Images from Live Scans.
  • Biometric Feature Vectors and Templates.
  • Liveliness Signals.
  • Similarity and Duplicate Face Indicators.
  • Age Estimation Outputs and Scores.

2. Why We Collect

We collect this data to accurately estimate your age so we can assess if you are old enough to use our services as outlined in our Terms of Service. This information is also collected to build a profile on the user ("you", "your", or "yours") to know, including but not limited to:

  • How many Age Gates were triggered and why.
  • To manually review your age via face scans and data.

3. How Data is Collected.

Data is collected only during verification via user-submitted live facial scans. This process is controlled and regulated by Didit. See section 5 for information on Didit and their role in age estimation.

4. Consent and Legal Basis.

Consent is obtained in multiple ways. When joining and interacting with our services, the user provides consent by agreeing to our Terms of Service and Privacy Policy. When a verification link is made and clicked (by the user) there is a consent page that requires the user to click on a check box, then the "Continue Button" to verify their age.

Verification is required for access to the services. If the user refuses to complete the verification process, they may not use our services. Likewise, if a user has a result that detects them as being under 13 years of age, they may not use the services offered by us.

5. Third Parties and Their Role.

Below, you will find a list of third parties that we use for age verification, along with their purpose

Name: Didit®
Role: Vendor for Age estimation, Liveliness Checks, and anything else relating to Age Verification via Face.
Privacy Policy: https://didit.me/terms/privacy-policy/

Note that each third party will have its own Privacy Policies; it is up to the user to read them as they are subject to them alongside our legal documentation (as appropriate).

We receive the following information from Didit for each verification session:

  • Overall verification decision status
  • Verification feature list used in the decision
  • Liveness check status
  • Liveness score
  • Liveness method type
  • Face quality score
  • Face luminance or lighting score
  • Liveness warnings or risk flags
  • Age estimation value or age range
  • Age estimation confidence indicator
  • Duplicate face detection flag
  • Duplicate face risk status
  • Prior match count
  • Face similarity scores against prior sessions
  • Match status per prior session
  • Blocklist hit flag
  • Prior matched session identifiers
  • Prior matched session numbers
  • Prior verification timestamps
  • Check or node identifiers
  • Verification timestamps
  • Session identifier
  • Session sequence number
  • Vendor media references for captured face image
  • Vendor media references for captured video, if collected
  • Structured warning and risk log entries tied to the session
  • URLs to the reference image used for verification
  • URLs to the matched images from prior sessions

7. Use Cases

We use all this information to build a profile on you for future verifications, and to prevent fraud, verify your age, and enforce the rules of Discord®.

8. Retention

We retain the information described in Section 6 indefinitely, unless a user requests deletion. If we receive a data deletion request from the user, then we will delete all verification sessions (open, in review, or closed, regardless of status) associated with the user's Discord User ID and Face via the dashboard within Didit®.

i. Deletion Triggers

We will delete appropriate verification data when legally required, such as via a deletion request or when the retention period expires.

9. Data Sharing

All verification data is shared with Didit® automatically, as they are the vendor who processes verifications (as outlined in section 5.) Authorized staff may access a user's age group (<13, 13–17, or 18+) and verification completion status.

i. Law Enforcement Requests

We will share all required data to law enforcement if required by court order or other legally compelling document.

10. Safeguards

We take your data seriously, which is why only our Director has access to databases containing verification data. Additional safeguards are maintained by Didit®.

11. User Rights

Where applicable, users have the right to do any of the following unless stated otherwise by a court order, new U.S law, or our Terms of Service:

  • Request Data Deletion
  • See what data has been collected so far.
  • Re-request / Request verification via a different form of verification that does not involve biometric data.

Please note that one or more of these options are at the discretion of Allegheny County Roleplay, while other information is a legal right or requirement. The following is a legal requirement:

  • Request Data Deletion
  • See what data has been collected so far.
  • Request data correction (if the user is in the EU and can articulate proof of residence in the EU)

12. Refusal Consequences.

If a user refuses to complete verification in some form, then their access to the services will be severely limited or revoked in some cases at our sole discretion.

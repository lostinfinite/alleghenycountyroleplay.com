Mental Health Resources

Updated: February 9, 2026

Support and resources for mental health, crisis intervention, and well-being. If you or someone you know is struggling, help is available.

Your mental health matters. If you or someone you know is struggling, please know that help is available. Below are some trusted resources and hotlines for mental health support, crisis intervention, and information.

Immediate Help (24/7 Hotlines)

  • 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline (US): Call or text 988 or visit 988lifeline.org
  • Crisis Text Line (US/Canada/UK): Text HOME to 741741 or visit crisistextline.org
  • National Alliance on Mental Illness (NAMI): Call 1-800-950-NAMI (6264) or visit nami.org/help
  • Trevor Project (LGBTQ+ Youth): Call 1-866-488-7386 or text START to 678678 or visit thetrevorproject.org

Online Resources

  • mentalhealth.gov – US government info and resources
  • National Institute of Mental Health: Find Help
  • SAMHSA National Helpline
  • Befrienders Worldwide – International crisis helplines

What To Do In a Crisis

  • If you or someone you know is in immediate danger, call 911 (US) or your local emergency number.
  • Reach out to a trusted friend, family member, or counselor.
  • Use the hotlines above for confidential, free support.

Remember

  • You are not alone. Many people experience mental health challenges.
  • Seeking help is a sign of strength, not weakness.
  • Support is available—please reach out if you need it.
